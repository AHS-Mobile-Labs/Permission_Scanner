PRIVACY POLICY - Permission Scanner
Last Updated: April 2026

=============================================================================
1. INTRODUCTION
=============================================================================

Permission Scanner ("the App") is a privacy-first Android application
designed to help users understand what permissions their installed apps
request and whether those permissions are justified.

This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you use
Permission Scanner. We are committed to protecting your privacy and
operating with transparency.

AHS Mobile Labs ("we", "us", "our") takes your privacy seriously. This
policy describes our practices regarding data collection, use, and storage.


=============================================================================
2. DATA WE COLLECT AND STORE LOCALLY
=============================================================================

Permission Scanner is designed to operate entirely on your device with
minimal data collection. All scanning and analysis happens locally.

2.1 LOCALLY STORED DATA

The App collects and stores the following information ONLY on your device:

  • Installed Apps List: Package names, app names, icon data, and install
    sources (detected from your device's package manager)
  
  • App Permissions: The list of permissions each installed app declares
    in its manifest (android.permission.*)
  
  • Risk Analysis: Locally computed risk levels and privacy scores based
    on permission counts
  
  • User Verifications: App capabilities you manually tag (e.g., "Takes
    Photos", "Records Audio") to help identify justified permissions
  
  • Cache Data: Fingerprints of your installed apps to optimize rescans
    and avoid redundant native calls
  
  • Scan History: Per-app permission scan records with timestamps and
    permission change tracking (stored in Hive database)

All of this data is stored ONLY in Hive local key-value storage on your
device and is never transmitted to external servers.


2.2 SYSTEM PERMISSIONS REQUIRED

To function, Permission Scanner requires these Android permissions:

  • QUERY_ALL_PACKAGES: To read the permission manifests of all installed
    apps on your device
  
  • POST_NOTIFICATIONS: To send optional local alerts when a newly
    installed app requests a high number of dangerous permissions

Both permissions are requested with clear justification when needed, and
notification permission is requested AFTER the main UI loads to avoid
blocking app startup.


2.3 DATA YOU DO NOT SEND TO US

Permission Scanner does NOT:

  • Send your installed app list to any server
  • Send your device identifiers (IMEI, serial number, or Android ID)
  • Transmit personal data, location, or network information
  • Connect to cloud services or external APIs
  • Share data with third parties
  • Include telemetry, analytics, or crash reporting


=============================================================================
3. HOW WE USE YOUR DATA
=============================================================================

3.1 LOCAL ANALYSIS

All data collected is used exclusively for:

  • Analyzing permissions requested by your installed apps
  • Calculating privacy scores and risk levels
  • Identifying potentially unjustified permission use
  • Building the permission database and UI displays
  • Storing your manual app verifications

3.2 CACHING AND OPTIMIZATION

Permission Scanner uses intelligent caching to:

  • Avoid redundant native calls by comparing app fingerprints
  • Provide instant app list loading on return visits
  • Store historical permission scan data for comparison over time

3.3 LOCAL NOTIFICATIONS

When enabled, we send local notifications only to:

  • Alert you when a newly installed app requests many dangerous
    permissions
  • Remind you of key privacy insights

These notifications are generated locally and never leave your device.


=============================================================================
4. DATA RETENTION
=============================================================================

4.1 HOW LONG WE KEEP DATA

  • Installed Apps List: Kept until manually cleared or app is uninstalled
  
  • Scan History: Last 30 permission scans per app are retained; older
    records are automatically removed
  
  • User Verifications: Stored indefinitely until manually deleted by you
  
  • Cache Metadata: Maintained for optimization until cleared
  
  • Device Fingerprints: Used only for change detection and discarded when
    no longer needed

4.2 CLEARING YOUR DATA

You can clear all cached data at any time using the "Clear Cache" action
in the Security Dashboard. You can also uninstall the app to remove all
stored data.

Uninstalling Permission Scanner deletes all:

  • Cached app information
  • Scan history records
  • User-created app verifications
  • Notification preferences


=============================================================================
5. OFFLINE-FIRST OPERATION
=============================================================================

Permission Scanner is designed to work fully offline:

  • No internet connection required for core functionality
  • No cloud synchronization
  • No server-side processing or storage
  • All computation happens on your device
  • The app respects the INTERNET permission but does not require it

This offline-first design ensures maximum privacy and minimal data exposure.


=============================================================================
6. PERMISSION JUSTIFICATION ENGINE
=============================================================================

The App includes a justification engine that maps app capabilities to
required permissions:

  • You manually select what features an app uses (e.g., "Take Photos")
  
  • The App matches these capabilities to Android permissions
  
  • Permissions supported by your tagged capabilities are marked as
    "justified"
  
  • This is a local reference tool and does not validate actual app
    behavior—it only helps you understand why permissions exist

The justification database is stored locally and never transmitted.


=============================================================================
7. SECURITY AND ENCRYPTION
=============================================================================

7.1 LOCAL STORAGE SECURITY

  • App data is stored in Hive, a secure local key-value database
  
  • Data is persisted in your app's private storage directory (accessible
    only by Permission Scanner and the system)
  
  • On devices with encrypted storage, Hive data benefits from device-level
    encryption

7.2 ICON CACHING

App icons are cached locally as base64-encoded image data and stored in
the Hive database. These icons are extracted from your device's installed
packages and are never transmitted externally.

7.3 NO EXTERNAL TRANSMISSION

Because Permission Scanner operates entirely offline, no encryption of
transmissions is needed—your data is never sent over the internet.

8. THIRD-PARTY LIBRARIES AND DEPENDENCIES
=============================================================================

Permission Scanner uses the following open-source packages:

  • flutter_riverpod: State management (no data collection)
  • hive & hive_flutter: Local storage (no external communication)
  • fl_chart: Data visualization (no data collection)
  • flutter_local_notifications: Local push notifications (device-only)
  • permission_handler: Android permission request handling (device-only)
  • intl: Date formatting (no data collection)

None of these libraries transmit user data to external servers. You can
verify this by reviewing their source code:

  https://pub.dev/packages/flutter_riverpod
  https://pub.dev/packages/hive
  https://pub.dev/packages/fl_chart

10. YOUR PRIVACY CHOICES
=============================================================================

10.1 NOTIFICATION PERMISSIONS

You can choose to grant or deny notification permissions at any time. If
denied, Permission Scanner will not send alerts about newly installed
high-risk apps but will continue to function normally.

10.2 DATA DELETION

You can delete all app data by:

  • Using the "Clear Cache" button in the Security Dashboard
  • Uninstalling the App
  • Using Android Settings > Apps > Permission Scanner > Storage > Clear
    Storage

10.3 PERMISSION REVOCATION

At any time, you can revoke the following permissions via Android Settings:

  • QUERY_ALL_PACKAGES (allows the App to see installed apps)
  • POST_NOTIFICATIONS (allows push notifications)

Revoking QUERY_ALL_PACKAGES will prevent the App from functioning properly,
but you can do this if you choose not to use the App.
